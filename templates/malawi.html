Project Malawi

This project came as a result of a 10-day visit to Malawi by Gideon and Kevin in November 2023 as part of the Greenfield Africa’s research and project assessment mission.

In order to provide a desirable and professional community development projects, Greenfield has entered into a partnership with a British-ran NGO, African Vision Malawi (AVM), which is registered under the laws of Malawi. AVM is involved in a number of humanitarian activities which include but not limited to provision of educational and healthcare delivery infrastructure, training of young adults in various vocational skills and agriculture empowerment activities. AVM has a famous training centre called Sam’s Village, Mteza, which is built on a 17-acre piece of land and about 25 minutes drive from Kamuzu International Airport. It has comfortable accommodation for visiting volunteers to stay at, while undertaking their various project placements.

Currently, Greenfield Africa provide opportunity for volunteers to participate in education, building and sporting activities in the villages nearby Sam’s Village. Teaching involves assisting children from the age of 2 to 15 years of age to understand basic English, Mathematics and general academic subjects. Building placement involves providing physical labour in building and refurbishing school buildings, such as classrooms, toilets, etc. Finally, sport and coaching placement entails teaching various kinds of sporting activities such as netball, volleyball and football that the schools undertake.
